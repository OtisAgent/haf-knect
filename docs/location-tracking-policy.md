# HAF KNECT — Driver Location & Tracking Policy

**Version 0.1 (internal draft) · 15 July 2026 · Owner: HAF Logistics Ltd (useHAF)**

This is a short, working policy for how HAF uses driver location on the KNECT platform. It is written to be read by a driver, a customer, and a solicitor. Review before enabling anything beyond what is described here.

## 1. Purpose
To let a customer follow their delivery in real time (a "live tracker"), and to plan and improve HAF's courier coverage — while treating drivers' location fairly and lawfully.

## 2. What we collect, and why
- **Live location during an active job** — a driver's current position, shared from their phone, only while they have the tracking screen open and sharing switched on. Used to show the customer where their delivery is.
- **Job postcodes** — the collection and delivery postcodes on each booking. Used for routing, coverage and network planning. This is ordinary business/booking data, not personal tracking.

## 3. What we do NOT do (current position)
- We do **not** keep a history or trail of a driver's movements. The live position is transient and is cleared once the delivery is complete.
- We do **not** track drivers when they are not on an active HAF job.
- We do **not** sell location data, or share it with anyone beyond the customer of that specific job.
- Internal planning uses **job postcodes only**, not personal location history.

## 4. Who can see it
- The **customer/business** for a job sees the driver's live position for that job (by driver name — never the driver's phone number).
- **HAF** sees operational tracking for jobs in progress.
- No third parties.

## 5. Retention
- **Live position:** transient — held only while the job is live, removed on completion.
- **Job postcodes:** kept with the normal booking record, under HAF's standard business-records retention.

## 6. Lawful basis (UK GDPR)
- Performance of the driver's delivery work, and HAF's **legitimate interest** in operating and improving the courier network. Sharing is voluntary and can be stopped at any time; consent is not relied on as the sole basis, given the working relationship.

## 7. Driver rights & control
- Sharing is **voluntary** — a driver can stop at any time by tapping Stop or closing the page; nothing is shared while it is off.
- Drivers can ask what data HAF holds about them, and request erasure, at any time.

## 8. Data controller & contact
HAF Logistics Ltd (useHAF). Data questions, access requests and erasure: **privacy@usehaf.co.uk**.

## 9. Change control — read before expanding
If HAF later decides to **retain a movement trail** (continuous GPS history) for analytics or "system betterment", that is a materially different, higher-risk use of worker location data. Before switching it on:
1. Complete a short **Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)**.
2. Set and enforce a **defined retention period** with automatic deletion.
3. Update this policy and the driver-facing terms to say so plainly.
4. Get a **solicitor's review** before go-live.

*The technical capability to store a trail exists in the platform but is switched OFF. It must not be enabled without the four steps above.*
